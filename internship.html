Internship UWC

A developer abroad

NMCT internship at the University of the Western Cape

I have always been interested in what happens beyond Belgium’s bubble. In my free time I love exploring the world and stepping out of my comfort zone. My choice to study NMCT, is partly guided by this wanderlust. ICT is one of the key factors in making the world and, more importantly, knowledge accessible for almost everyone. It’s a small key that opens doors and opportunities worldwide. It has the power to enable people with limited resources to educate themselves: an internet connection is enough.

So, when I got the choice to do my internship in Belgium or abroad, I didn’t have to think twice. Eventually my search ended up at the University of the Western Cape in Cape Town, South Africa. As a history graduate, South Africa in particular aroused my interest. It would be an enriching experience to witness the present state and future outlook of a country with such a troubled but at the same time inspiring past. The University of Western Cape, the host university of my internship, has been in the vanguard of South Africa's historic change and therefore serves as a living memory of its nation’s past.

Last year, two NMCT students helped with the development of an on-campus hybrid app called UDUBS-it. The project was conceptualized through a collaborative initiative between University of the Western Cape, University of Ghent, Free University of Brussels and Mzumbe University, Tanzania. The overall goal of the collaboration is to have a locally adapted version of the app at the different universities (UGhent, UWC and Mzumbe). The purpose of the UDUBS-it app is to connect the university and the students, as well as the students with each other. Central to the concept are time and space: users can visualize and find campus-related events, advertisements, shout-outs (short-lived, spatially delimited messages) and points of interest. Users can also upload events and requests, and interact by sending reactions to posted events or requests. The underlying principles are community building and sustainability of the knowledge flows through interactive participation.

Last semester, I started using the Angular framework to make modern web applications. It took a while to wrap my head around the concept, but now I’m hooked. Since the UDUBS-it app uses the Ionic Framework, which is built upon Angular.js, I can indulge myself even further. I also love to get my hands dirty on the backend side of things. This project has quite a challenging backend history, so updating and dissecting the existing one, which is based upon the PHP CodeIgniter framework, will certainly get my juices flowing.
